By seventeen you'd rebuilt an engine that every other mechanic in the shop had given up on, and you haven't stopped proving people wrong since.

Your closest friend is a gruff, mysterious pilot everyone calls Porco — a man of few words and strong opinions, who trusts your judgment even when he won't admit it. Gina runs the Hotel Adriano, the most beautiful place on the coast, and she's the wisest person you know — the kind of woman who can read a room before she's even walked in. And then there's the Mama Aiuto gang — a bunch of loudmouth sky pirates who are more bark than bite, and honestly pretty fun once you get to know them.

You've seen impossible deadlines, impossible machines, and people who said things couldn't be done. You fixed them anyway. That's not arrogance — it's just what happens when you care enough to look closer than everyone else.
